Managing construction defects during the warranty period has traditionally relied on spreadsheets, phone calls, and WhatsApp groups. This fragmented approach leads to lost information, delayed repairs, and frustrated residents.

## The Problem with Manual Tracking

Most construction companies still track defects using Excel spreadsheets shared via email. Inspectors write handwritten notes, managers re-enter data, and residents have no visibility into the process. Information gets lost, duplicated, or outdated.

## Digital Transformation in Defect Management

Modern platforms like Fixeet bring structure to the chaos. Every defect is logged with photos and videos, assigned to the right tradesperson, scheduled for repair, and tracked to verified completion. All stakeholders see the same real-time status.

## What This Means for the Industry

The shift from manual to digital defect management reduces repair cycles by up to 40%, cuts unnecessary site visits, and dramatically improves resident satisfaction. Companies that adopt these tools gain a competitive advantage in customer retention.
