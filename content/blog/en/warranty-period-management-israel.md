The construction warranty period (תקופת בדק) in Israel presents unique challenges that generic project management tools weren't designed to handle. With thousands of new apartments delivered annually, the scale of defect management is enormous.

## The Israeli Bedek Period

Israeli construction law mandates a warranty period after apartment handover during which the construction company must repair defects. This period varies by defect type — from one year for minor issues to ten years for structural problems.

## Stakeholder Complexity

The bedek process involves multiple parties with different needs: apartment owners who want fast repairs, construction companies managing hundreds of units, subcontractors juggling multiple sites, and inspectors documenting everything. Communication typically happens through fragmented WhatsApp groups.

## Regulatory Requirements

Israeli building regulations require detailed documentation of defect reports, repair timelines, and completion verification. Manual processes make compliance burdensome and error-prone.

## Technology as the Solution

Digital platforms designed specifically for the bedek period can address these challenges by providing a single source of truth, automated notifications, compliance-ready documentation, and real-time visibility for all stakeholders.
