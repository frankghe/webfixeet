Repeat visits are one of the biggest cost drivers in construction defect management. Every unnecessary return trip wastes contractor time, delays other repairs, and frustrates residents. Here are five proven strategies to get it right the first time.

## 1. Complete Task Lists Before Arrival

Ensure every tradesperson has a detailed, prioritized task list with photos and descriptions before they arrive on-site. No more discovering issues only after showing up.

## 2. Photo and Video Documentation

Require visual documentation of every defect at the time of reporting. This helps tradespeople understand the issue before they arrive and bring the right tools and materials.

## 3. Coordinate Multiple Trades per Visit

When possible, schedule related trades for the same visit window. A plumber and tiler working together can resolve a bathroom leak and its resulting damage in one visit instead of two.

## 4. Pre-Visit Material Verification

Confirm that all required materials are available before scheduling a repair visit. Nothing wastes more time than arriving on-site only to discover a missing part.

## 5. Digital Sign-Off and Verification

Use a digital platform to verify repairs on completion. Photo evidence of the fix, signed off by the inspector, prevents disputes and repeat inspections.
